## 1. Two-Body Gravitational Dynamics
The isolated interaction of a test mass $m$ orbiting a dominant central mass $M$ forms the baseline of the orbital frame. The Governing differential equation of motion is given by Newton's law of universal gravitation:

$$\ddot{\vec{r}} + \frac{G(M+m)}{r^3}\vec{r} = 0$$

Where $\vec{r}$ represents the position vector of the orbiting mass relative to the system barycenter.

## 2. The Vis-Viva Equation
Energy conservation dictating the velocity profile along any conic section trajectory is formalized through the Vis-Viva relation:

$$v^2 = \mu \left( \frac{2}{r} - \frac{1}{a} \right)$$

Where:
* $v$ is the relative orbital speed of the body.
* $\mu = G(M+m)$ is the standard gravitational parameter.
* $r$ is the instantaneous scalar distance between the bodies.
* $a$ is the semi-major axis of the orbit ($a > 0$ for ellipses, $a \to \infty$ for parabolas, $a < 0$ for hyperbolas).
