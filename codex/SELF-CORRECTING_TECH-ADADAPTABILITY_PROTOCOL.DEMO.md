# SELF‑CORRECTING & TECHNOLOGICAL ADAPTABILITY PROTOCOL
# Version: 1.0
# Realm: DEMO
# Status: Public Mirror (Non‑Canonical)

This DEMO version mirrors the ROOT protocol for public reference.
It carries no governance authority and is not checksum‑binding.

---

## 1. PURPOSE
To demonstrate how the System conceptually:
- observes itself,
- detects drift or obsolescence,
- proposes corrections,
- adapts to new technologies,
while remaining under explicit human governance.

---

## 2. CORE PRINCIPLES
- Human Primacy  
- Transparency  
- Reversibility  
- Non‑Autonomy in Critical Domains  

---

## 3. OBSERVATION LAYER
Non‑autonomous monitoring of:
- deprecated libraries,
- compatibility issues,
- security advisories,
- performance degradation.

Outputs **Change Candidate Records (CCR)**.

---

## 4. ANALYSIS & PROPOSAL LAYER
Each CCR becomes a **Change Proposal (CP)** with:
- impact classification,
- recommended change,
- rationale,
- risks,
- human‑readable summary.

---

## 5. HUMAN GOVERNANCE LAYER
All CPs require:
- human review,
- explicit approval,
- logged decisions.

No autonomous execution.

---

## 6. IMPLEMENTATION & VERIFICATION
Approved CPs undergo:
- implementation planning,
- staging/testing,
- verification,
before optional deployment.

---

## 7. TECHNOLOGICAL ADAPTABILITY RULES
New technologies undergo:
- evaluation,
- sandbox pilot,
- gradual adoption.

System may **suggest**, never **enforce**.

---

## 8. INTEGRITY & AUDIT
All CCRs, CPs, and decisions are:
- timestamped,
- versioned,
- auditable.

Periodic human audits required.

---

## 9. SAFETY & BOUNDARIES
This protocol does **not** grant authority to:
- move funds,
- sign contracts,
- alter legal records,
- control infrastructure.

---

## 10. META‑CORRECTION
The protocol itself may be amended only through:
- human review,
- documented approval,
- version increment.
