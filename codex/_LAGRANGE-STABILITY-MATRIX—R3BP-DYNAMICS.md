## 1. Rotating Coordinate System
Normalizing the distance between the two heavy primaries to 1, the total mass to 1, and the gravitational constant $G$ to 1 transforms the system into a synodic rotating coordinate frame where the primary masses occupy fixed points on the x-axis:
* $m_1$ positioned at $(-\mu, 0, 0)$
* $m_2$ positioned at $(1-\mu, 0, 0)$

## 2. Effective Potential Functions ($U$)
The kinetic equilibrium of the third mass is dictated by the combination of gravitational potential fields and rotational centrifugal forces:

$$U(x, y, z) = \frac{1}{2}(x^2 + y^2) + \frac{1-\mu}{r_1} + \frac{\mu}{r_2}$$

Where:
* $r_1 = \sqrt{(x + \mu)^2 + y^2 + z^2}$
* $r_2 = \sqrt{(x - 1 + \mu)^2 + y^2 + z^2}$
